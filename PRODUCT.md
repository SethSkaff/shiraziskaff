# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, weighted roughly equally:

**Early users / beta testers** — people who discover Coach, AirMix, or Crave through social, word of mouth, or search. They are evaluating whether the product fits a real frustration they have right now. They are not technical. They want to understand what the product does in one scroll and decide whether to sign up or join a waitlist.

**Investors and advisors** — sophisticated readers sizing up the studio thesis and the founders. They are asking: is the vertical AI bet credible, are these the right people to make it, and is each product narrow enough to be believable? They read the copy carefully and notice when founders hedge.

## Product Purpose

Shirazi Skaff Enterprises is a vertical AI products studio. Its three current products — Coach (real-time math tutoring via camera), AirMix (AR DJ board, $3.99/month), Crave (AI nutrition coach that rewrites meals from photos) — share a single thesis: pick a service people already pay for, understand the behavioral loop behind it, and build software that delivers the outcome with more immediacy and lower cost.

The website exists to make that thesis legible and to drive two actions: product signups and investor credibility.

## Brand Personality

Precise, intentional, direct. Referencing Linear, Vercel, Stripe: confidence through restraint, not through decoration. The copy is the brand — short declarative sentences, no hedging, no hype. Young founders with sharp ideas, not with loud identity.

## Anti-references

- **YC-batch SaaS**: purple gradients, hero metric counters, "Join 10,000 teams", social proof walls. Generic startup template.
- **AI hype**: neon on black, brain/node icons, words like "revolutionary", "next-gen", or "powered by AI" as a selling point.
- **Agency portfolio**: oversized type, slow scroll animations for their own sake, case-study grid layouts. SSE is a product studio, not a design house.
- **Consumer-app warmth**: pastel palettes, rounded-bubble UI, emoji-heavy copy, anything that reads as soft or playful.

## Design Principles

1. **The copy is the product.** Every heading must make a claim. If a section heading could appear on any startup's site, rewrite it until it couldn't.
2. **Narrow confidence.** Do not oversell. "Math tutoring first" is more credible than "AI for education." Specificity is the design move.
3. **Trust through constraint.** Restraint in color, motion, and decoration signals that the founders know what they are doing. Decoration reads as covering for weakness.
4. **Each product earns its own identity.** Coach, AirMix, and Crave have distinct color palettes for a reason — they are separate products, not feature tabs. Design should reinforce that.
5. **The site is an argument.** A reader should finish the home page understanding the thesis, not just the product names. Structure and hierarchy serve the argument, not the aesthetic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. No specific known user needs beyond standard. Reduced motion support expected (the site uses entrance animations). Color contrast should hold for all three product accent colors against the warm off-white base.
